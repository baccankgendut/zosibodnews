Is Derrick Rose Muslim? Instagram post makes basketball fans believe that he converted to Islam

Back in 2020, Rose published an Instagram post regarding his 11th signature shoe with Adidas. The point guard also mentioned Allah in the post, which is why many of his followers started thinking that he converted to Islam.

While there was no official confirmation of Rose's conversion, a lot of basketball fans incorrectly assumed a big change in his life. However, it doesn't appear that Rose has converted to Islam.

"Allah/Universe already got the plan," Derrick Rose wrote in the caption of his Instagram post.

Despite his mention of Allah, Derrick Rose is not Muslim. In fact, he's still a Christian and has spoken about his faith several times. The veteran guard also has several religious tattoos, including "God's Child" on his back.

The 3-time All-Star also wears a wristband that says "In Jesus' Name I Play." While Rose seems to be a devout Christian, his denomination is still a mystery.

More information about Derrick Rose's background and life

The veteran point guard has a mixed ethnicity. He was born in Chicago, Illinois, the city that drafted him in 2008. Rose was also raised in the Windy City, but he left once he decided to join the University of Memphis.

Rose's mother, Brenda Rose, raised him alone with three more brothers. All of his brothers were talented and they helped Derrick learn how to play the game.

At the University of Memphis, Rose established himself as one of the best young players in the nation. He led the Tigers to the national championship game, but couldn't beat the Kansas Jayhawks.
